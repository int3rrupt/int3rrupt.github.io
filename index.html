About

I was born in Uriangato, Guanajuato, Mexico in 1986. When I was two, my family and I moved to California.

I was first exposed to computers in elementary school while playing Oregon Trail. When I was nine, my dad purchased the family's first PC, an IBM Aptiva running Windows 95. I've had a passion for technology ever since.

RC Magnetic Levitation Vehicle

Magnetically levitated vehicle that achieves lift by alternating magnetic fields over a conductor. Alternating magnetic fields induce Eddy currents in the conductor which in turn create an opposing magnetic field resulting in levitation.

  • Role: Team Lead
  • Date: May 2016

Campus Ride

Campus Ride is a mobile application that allows students on campus to request rides to any other location on the campus in exchange for Ride Points. Students are also encouraged to give rides in order to earn Ride Points that they can then use for rides. Developed for both Android and iOS (Not yet released).

Originally developed for Cal Poly's CS 480 Software Engineering course with plans for improvement and a future release.

  • Team: 4
  • Role: Web API Backend, Linux Server, Deployment, Continuous Integration
